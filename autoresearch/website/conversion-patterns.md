# Conversion Patterns & Anti-Slop — Site Optimization Notes

## From the 4 Landing Page Architecture Images

### Common Pattern Across ALL 4 (consensus structure):
1. **Navbar** — sticky, minimal links + CTA button
2. **Hero** — headline + subheadline + CTA + trust signals/social proof
3. **Social Proof / Partners** — logos, user count, ratings
4. **Problem-Agitate** — make status quo painful (Old Way vs New Way)
5. **Benefits** — benefits sell, features tell. Focus on HOW it HELPS, not what it HAS
6. **How It Works** — 3 simple steps
7. **Features** — highlighted features with bento/card layout
8. **Pricing** — clear tiers, highlight middle plan
9. **Testimonials** — 3 with specific results, names, roles
10. **FAQ** — accordion, reduce hesitation
11. **Secondary CTA** — catch the scrollers
12. **Footer** — logo, nav, legal, social

### Key Conversion Principles:
- Each section has ONE JOB:
  - Hero → get email or get scroll
  - Problem → make status quo painful
  - Social proof → let others convince them
  - Value stack → make saying no feel stupid
  - CTA → catch the scrollers
- **Trust signals EARLY** (hero area or just below)
- **Comparison section** (Old Way vs New Way) is powerful for GT/Cortex
- **Transformation section** — show the journey: Quick Win → Compound → 10x

## Anti-Slop Guidelines (from humanizer + stop-slop)

### Words/Phrases to ELIMINATE from site copy:
- "Revolutionize", "Transform", "Game-changer", "Cutting-edge"
- "Seamlessly", "Effortlessly", "Unlock the power of"
- "Dive into", "Navigate the landscape of"
- "It's not just X, it's Y" (negative parallelism)
- "From X to Y" (false ranges)
- "Additionally", "Furthermore", "Moreover"
- "Nestled within", "Boasts", "Showcases"
- Em dash overuse
- Rule of three clichés ("innovation, inspiration, and insights")

### What GOOD copy sounds like:
- Direct statements, not announcements
- Specific, not vague ("2,400 users" not "thousands of users")
- Active voice, short sentences
- Respects reader intelligence (don't over-explain)
- Sounds like a person talking, not a press release
- Real numbers, real examples, real pain points
